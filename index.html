The reality

Everyone's on their own ChatGPT. Nothing talks to anything else.

Most agencies have people using AI — but each person is in their own silo. Their own account, their own prompts, their own context. Intelligence isn't being shared. Nothing compounds. And the business never gets the full benefit.

Some agencies try to fix this with training sessions on how to prompt better. But that's treating the symptom, not the problem. The issue isn't how individuals use AI — it's that there's no connected infrastructure underneath.

Fragmented tools

No clear strategy

Wasted investment
